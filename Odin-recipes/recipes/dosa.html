Dosa

Description

Dosa is a popular South Indian thin, savoury, and crispy crepe made from a fermented batter of ground rice and black gram (urad dal), usually served hot with chutney and sambar. It is a versatile, nutrient-rich dish—providing carbohydrates and protein—often enjoyed for breakfast or as a snack

Ingredients

  • Rice: 3 cups (Idli rice or parboiled rice)
  • Urad Dal: 1 cup (Whole or split skinned white lentil)
  • Fenugreek Seeds (Methi): 1/2 tsp (Optional, for fermentation and softness)
  • Poha (Flattened Rice): 2 tbsp (Optional, for added crispiness)
  • Salt: To taste (approx. 1 tsp)
  • Water: As needed for soaking and grinding
  • Oil/Ghee: For cooking

Steps for making Dosa

  1. Soak: Wash the rice and urad dal (with methi seeds) separately, then soak them in water for at least 4–6 hours.
  2. Grind: Drain the water and grind the soaked ingredients separately (or together) into a smooth, thick batter, adding water only as needed.
  3. Mix & Ferment: Combine the batters in a large bowl, mix well with your hand, cover, and let it ferment in a warm place for 8–12 hours until doubled in size.
  4. Prep: Add salt to the fermented batter and stir it gently to combine.
  5. Heat: Heat a non-stick or cast-iron tawa (griddle) over medium heat and lightly grease it with oil.
  6. Spread: Pour a ladleful of batter in the center and spread it in a circular motion to form a thin circle.
  7. Cook: Drizzle a little oil or ghee around the edges and cook until the bottom is golden brown and crisp.
  8. Serve: Lift the crispy dosa from the tawa and serve it hot with chutney and sambar.
