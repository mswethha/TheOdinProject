Idly

Description

Idli (or idly) is a popular, nutritious South Indian savory rice cake made by steaming a fermented batter of rice and black lentils (urad dal). Known for being soft, fluffy, low-fat, and healthy, these cakes are typically served hot with sambar and chutney for breakfast

Ingredients

  • Idli Rice (Parboiled Rice): 2 cups (best for soft texture).
  • Urad Dal (Split Skinned Black Gram): 1 cup.
  • Fenugreek Seeds (Methi): 1/2 to 1 tsp (crucial for fermentation and aroma).
  • Water: For soaking and grinding (use chilled water while grinding to keep the mix cool).
  • Salt: To taste (non-iodized sea salt is preferred).
  • Cooked Rice: 2 tbsp, blended with the rice.

Steps for making idlies

  1. Thoroughly wash the rice and urad dal separately 3-4 times until the water runs clear.
    Soak the rice in a large bowl. In a separate bowl, soak the urad dal along with the fenugreek seeds and, if using, the poha.
    Let them soak for at least 4 to 6 hours.
  2. Drain the water from the soaked urad dal and grind it in a wet grinder or high-speed blender until it becomes fluffy, light, and smooth. Add ice-cold water in small quantities while grinding to prevent the mixture from heating up, which can make the idlis dense.
    Drain the water from the rice and grind it to a slightly coarse, rava-like texture.
    Combine the ground rice and urad dal batter in a large container.
    Add salt to the mixture. Using clean hands, mix the batter thoroughly for 2-3 minutes. Hand-mixing helps incorporate air and aids in better fermentation.
  3. Cover the container with a lid (not airtight) and set it in a warm, dark place for 8-12 hours.
    The batter should double in volume and have a slightly sour, fermented smell.
  4. Heat 2 cups of water in an idli steamer or pressure cooker (without the whistle). Lightly grease the idli plates with oil.
    Gently stir the fermented batter (do not over-mix, or you will lose the air pockets) and ladle it into the moulds.
    Place the plates in the steamer and steam on medium-high heat for 10-12 minutes.
    Insert a knife or toothpick; if it comes out clean, the idlis are done.
    Let the idlis rest in the pan for 2-3 minutes before removing them. Serve hot with coconut chutney and sambar.
