Chappathi

Description

A chappathi is an unleavened, whole-wheat flatbread that originated in the Indian subcontinent and is now a daily staple across South Asia, East Africa, and the Caribbean. The name comes from the Hindi word chapat, meaning "slap," referencing the traditional method of shaping the dough by slapping it between the palms.

Ingredients

  • Atta: The main ingredient, which gives the chapati its texture and nutritional value.
  • Lukewarm water is typically used to help create a soft, pliable dough.
  • Oil or Ghee: Used in the dough for softness and applied on top during cooking for flavor and texture.
  • Salt: Added to taste, usually a small pinch.

Steps for making chapathi

  1. Dough: Mix 2 cups atta, pinch of salt, 1 tbsp oil, and 3/4 - 1 cup warm water.
  2. Knead & Rest: Knead for 5-7 mins until soft and non-sticky, then rest covered for 20-30 mins.
  3. Roll: Divide into 6-8 balls, dust with flour, and roll into 6-7 inch thin circles.
  4. Cook: Heat tawa to medium-high; cook for 30s, flip, cook 1 min, apply ghee/oil, and press gently to puff.
  5. Serve: Keep in a casserole or towel-lined container to stay soft.
